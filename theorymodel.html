Calculus Mathematics Sir Isaac Newton The branch of mathematics that deals with limits and the differentiation and integration of functions of one or more variables. Mid 17th Century With calculus, we have the ability to find the effects of changing conditions on a system. By studying these, you can learn how to control a system to make it do what you want it to do.
Theory of Evolution Biology Charles Darwin
  • A species is a population of organisms that interbreeds and has fertile offspring.
  • Living organisms have descended with modifications from species that lived before them.
  • More organisms are produced than can survive because of limited resources.
1859
  • Ecology
  • Artificial Selection
  • Medicine
Theory of Relativity Physics Albert Einstein The Laws of Physics are the same for all non-accelerating observers, and the speed of light is the same in a vacuum no matter what speed an observer travels. 1916 Einstein's general relativity theory says that gravity curves space and time, resulting in a tendency for the orbiting clocks to tick slightly faster, by about 45 microseconds per day. Without the proper application of relativity, GPS would fail in its navigational functions within about 2 minutes.
Dynamite Chemistry Alfred Nobel An explosive made of nitroglcerin, sorbents and stabilizers that is safer to use than other explosives such as gun powder. 1867 Dynamite has been in use since its creation for purposes such as leveling out-dated buildings in the demolition industry and blasting away rocks in the mining, construction and quarrying industries.
Turing Test Computer Science Alan Turing A test developed to see if a machine can exhibit intelligent behavior that is indistinguishable from that of a human being. 1950 Important concept in the philosophy of artificial intelligence.
Heliocentric Model Astronomy Galileo Galilei An astronomical model that has been pondered for centuries which posits that the Sun is at the center of our solar system and the Earth rotates around it. 1610 Slowly changed people's perspective of Earth's place in the universe, paved the way for future astronomical discoveries.
