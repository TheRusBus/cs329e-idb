Field Description History/Origin Subfields Impacts/Applications Recent Discoveries Prominent Scientists
Physics The study of matter and its motion and behavior. Dates back to ancient times. Formal study began in the 16th century. Classical Mechanics, Electrodynamics, Optics, Acoustics, Quantum Mechanics, Relativity Often motivates creation of new mathematics, and is used for the development of new technologies. Gravitational waves, the Higgs Boson Albert Einstein, Erwin Schrodinger, Isaac Newton
Mathematics A broad-ranging field of study in which the properties and interactions of idealized objects are examined. Dates back to ancient times. The progress of the field can be viewed as an ever increasing series of abstractions. Number theory, geometry, topology, analysis Used to formalize nearly every field of science. Used to facilitate computation in all quantitative fields. Bounded gaps between primes Leonhard Euler, Carl Gauss, Bernhard Riemann, Isaac Newton
Biology The study of life and organisms. The study of botany and zoology dates back to ancient times, but formal biological study began in the 1700s. Molecular Biology, Microbiology, Botany, Zoology, Ecology Widely used in medicine and agriculture. Mapping of the human genome Charles Darwin, Gregor Mendel, Robert Hooke
Chemistry The study of atoms and how they form compunds. Dates back to ancient times in the form of alchemy. Formal study began in the 1600s. Biochemistry, Organic Chemistry, Inorganic Chemistry, Physical Chemistry Widely used in medicine and agriculture. Used to develop new materials and chemicals. New food preservation options, titanium dioxide coating that reduces air pollution Dimitri Mendeleev, Marie Curie, John Dalton, Alfred Nobel
Astronomy The study of celestial objects and phenomena. The study of astronomy dates back ancient times, and it is often considered the oldest science. Planetary Science, Stellar Astronomy, Cosmology Advances in astronomical techniques often lead to new technologies. Used to calculate location and dates. Discovery of gravitational waves and Earth-like planets. Isaac Newton, Galileo Galilei, Nicolaus Copernicus, Johannes Kepler
Computer Science The study of the principles and uses of computers. Mechanical computation devices and programming methods originated in the 1800s. Theory of Computation, Algorithms and Data Structures, Programming Language Theory, Computer Elements and Architecture Advances in computation have led to new discoveries in science and have improved communication and quality of life. Significant improvement in computer hardware and programming techniques. Alan Turing, Donald Knuth
